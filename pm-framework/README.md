# PM Framework

This framework guides how I approach every product problem, from initial 
hypothesis through validated execution. It is methodology-first, evidence-based, 
and designed to eliminate the most common failure mode in product development: 
building the wrong thing with confidence.

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: Opportunity Hypothesis

Every product decision starts with a question, not an answer. Before a single 
feature is scoped or a line of code is written, I define the opportunity in 
falsifiable terms.

What I produce at this stage:
- Problem statement (two sentences maximum)
- Target persona with Jobs to Be Done mapping
- Assumed behavior that must be true for the opportunity to exist
- Success metric that would prove the hypothesis right or wrong

### Stage 2: Validate

Opinions are not facts. Less than 50% of product ideas hit their target metrics 
when shipped. Validation exists to kill bad ideas cheaply before they become 
expensive builds.

What I produce at this stage:
- Customer discovery interview guide
- Synthesized interview findings
- Validated or invalidated hypothesis with evidence
- Go / No-Go recommendation with reasoning

### Stage 3: Execute

Only validated opportunities get scoped. Execution artifacts exist to give 
engineers everything they need to build without ambiguity and give stakeholders 
everything they need to evaluate progress.

What I produce at this stage:
- Product Requirements Document (PRD) with acceptance criteria
- Prioritized roadmap with scoring rationale
- Compliance flags and open decisions log
- Definition of done

## Principles

Evidence over instinct. A data point beats an opinion every time. When data 
does not exist, name it as a hypothesis and design a test.

Constraints before solutions. Understanding what cannot change (compliance, 
architecture, budget, timeline) before designing what can.

Delta-only communication. Stakeholders get what
