# Persona Development

Personas are only useful if they are grounded in real behavior, not assumptions. 
Every persona I build starts with primary research and is structured around 
Jobs to Be Done, not demographics.

## How I Build Personas

Step 1: Define the research question. What decision or behavior am I trying to 
understand? Personas built without a research question become decoration.

Step 2: Conduct discovery interviews. Minimum 5 interviews per persona segment. 
I listen for language, frustration patterns, workarounds, and what people are 
already hiring other solutions to do.

Step 3: Synthesize findings. I map findings to four JTBD dimensions: functional 
job, emotional job, social job, and consumption chain.

Step 4: Validate assumptions. Any attribute that came from assumption rather than 
observed behavior gets flagged as a hypothesis until a second source confirms it.

Step 5: Connect to product decisions. A persona that does not directly inform a 
prioritization decision or acceptance criteria is not doing its job.

## Persona Template

**Name and Role**
A realistic composite name and the job title most common in this segment.

**The Problem They Hire For**
One sentence describing the functional outcome they are trying to achieve.

**Current Workaround**
What they are doing today instead of using your product. This is your real 
competition.

**Frustrations**
What is broken about the current workaround. Quoted language from interviews 
where possible.

**Success Looks Like**
How they would describe a perfect outcome in their own words.

**What They Fear**
The risk they are trying to avoid. Often the real reason they have not already 
solved the problem.

**Where They Spend Time**
Channels, communities, and tools. Informs GTM and messaging strategy.

## What I Do Not Include

Age ranges, stock photos, and fictional names that make personas feel like 
marketing exercises rather than product tools. Demographics that are not 
causally connected to the behavior being studied. Attributes that cannot be 
tested or observed.
